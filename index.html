The failure is a timing and information problem — not a job skill problem.

When a new hire misses a shift, the employer sees unreliability. What actually happened started 48–72 hours earlier: a car that wouldn't start, a childcare arrangement that collapsed, a financial shock that made getting to work impossible.

The workforce ecosystem responds reactively. Gas cards after a crisis. Ride vouchers after an absence. Emergency repairs after a breakdown strands someone. None of this spending is coordinated. Nearly all of it arrives too late.

The 72-hour window nobody monitors.

What We Believe

Six commitments. Written down. Non-negotiable.

These are not aspirations. They are design constraints — embedded in how Aryvl is built, how Maya speaks, and how every decision gets made. If we violate any of them, we have failed.

Working for you, not on you.
Every other system in your life reaches out when something is wrong. A bill, a warning, a consequence. We reach out when something is right, when something needs attention before you'd even know it, and when you've earned acknowledgment. This system exists to protect your stability. Full stop.
Consistency compounds.
Trust isn't built in one phone call. It's built by the same voice, the same tone, the same follow-through, week after week. For people who've been let down by programs that promised support and disappeared, showing up reliably is the intervention. We don't get credit for the first call. We earn it by the tenth.
Supporting, not surveilling.
We see what we need to see. Did the car start, did you get there safely, is something getting in the way. Nothing more. No location history. No recorded calls. No data that exists to watch you. What we learn, we use to help you. That's the only reason we're paying attention.
Never disciplinary. Never punitive.
We don't build cases. We don't generate evidence. We don't report problems up a chain that ends in consequences for you. Our job is to catch something early and get the right help in place before it becomes a crisis. If anything we do is ever used against a worker, we have failed at the most basic level.
Defined dignity.
Dignity isn't a value we list. It's a design constraint. It means we never scold. Never lecture. Never reference what you should have done. Never make you feel like a problem to be managed. Meeting you where you are isn't a compromise. It's the only way this works.
Your story. Your call.
What you share with us stays with us. When you enroll, we tell you exactly what gets passed along, to who, and why. No surprises, no exceptions. We share signals with your case manager so they can help. Never with your employer. Never with anyone outside the program. What stays private stays private. What gets shared, you already knew would be shared before you ever picked up the phone.
The Science — Why This Works

People disclose to AI what they won't tell humans. The research explains why this isn't surprising.

The workers Aryvl serves have learned that disclosure has consequences. They don't report instability to supervisors, case managers, or employers — because they've been burned before. Research on human-AI interaction reveals a structural advantage for voice AI in precisely this context.

↓ Fear
People experience significantly less fear of judgment when disclosing to AI than to a human interlocutor
An experimental study with 286 participants found that people experience meaningfully lower fear of judgment when talking to an AI. For populations that have been penalized for disclosure throughout their lives, this is a structural advantage — not a workaround.
Croes et al., "Digital Confessions," Interacting with Computers, Oxford Academic, Sept. 2024 (N=286)
= Benefits
Emotional benefits of disclosure are equivalent whether the listener is AI or human
In a controlled experiment, participants who disclosed to a chatbot experienced equivalent improvements in mood and perceived relationship quality as those who disclosed to a human — regardless of whether they knew they were talking to AI.
Ho et al., 2018; synthesized in PMC Review on Generative AI & Human Connection, 2024
↑ Openness
Human-like framing increases self-disclosure even when users consciously know it's AI
A controlled experiment found that participants disclosed more and felt more comforted when AI was given a human name and warm framing — even when nearly all participants correctly identified it as a bot. The effect is driven by framing design, not deception.
Warren-Smith, Laban et al., "Knowledge Cues to Human Origins," PsyPost, Nov. 2025
🔒 Trust
AI is perceived as inherently confidential — a critical trust advantage with populations who've been surveilled
Because AI lacks social ties and cannot share information with others, people view it as confidential by nature. For workers who have learned that disclosure to supervisors or case managers triggers compliance chains, this perceived confidentiality is load-bearing.
Skjuve & Brandtzæg, 2018; synthesized in Croes et al., Oxford Academic, 2024
"Rosa would never have told her case manager she was driving uninsured. She would never have told her employer. She told Maya because Maya isn't connected to either one — and because Maya asked the right question at the right time."

The voice agent isn't a survey tool. It's a relationship that compounds.

Weeks 1–2: Warm, brief, no pressure. The goal is to establish the habit of picking up the phone. Weeks 3–4: Gentle probing, near-miss detection, commute confidence ratings. Weeks 5–6: Normalized disclosure. Maya references prior conversations. Workers tell her the truth.

By week 6, the agent can ask "Last week you mentioned your brakes were squeaking — did you get that looked at?" and workers respond. That continuity is impossible with rotating case managers or monthly check-ins.

This is the only check-in channel that scales to weekly without adding headcount.

A human case manager with a 50-person caseload cannot deliver weekly check-ins, read vocal stress indicators, cross-reference vehicle telemetry, and route interventions to the right party — before the next shift starts.

The AI voice agent handles 80%+ of routine touchpoints. One coordinator handles the 20% of exception cases that require judgment. This is how you get the check-in density that drives 82% retention improvement at a unit cost that doesn't require grant dependency indefinitely.
