I woke myself up Because we ain't got an alarm clock Dug in the dirty clothes basket, Cause ain't nobody washed my uniform Brushed my hair and teeth in the dark, Cause the lights ain't on Even got my baby sister ready, Cause my mama wasn't home. Got us both to school on time, To eat us a good breakfast. Then when I got to class the teacher fussed Cause I ain't got no pencil.
— Joshua T. Dickerson, Baltimore student
A child overcame every obstacle between him and the classroom — and the system noticed only the one thing he couldn't fix alone. That child grows up. The alarm clock becomes a car that won't start. The uniform becomes a shift schedule no bus can reach. The dark house becomes a paycheck that doesn't cover both rent and repairs. And the pencil? The pencil becomes a missed shift — the only thing the employer sees. Aryvl is built for the adult version of this poem.
